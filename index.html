About me

My name is Daniel, and I am a Physics Engineering graduate currently pursuing my Master's degree at the University of Porto.

My primary focus lies at the intersection of theoretical and applied physics. I am deeply interested in classical physics, statistical mechanics, quantum mechanics, and solid-state physics, particularly regarding the magnetic properties of materials. To support this theoretical foundation, I actively apply computational and engineering tools, including data analysis, machine learning, electronics, and programming.

Previously, I completed an academic internship at the Faculty of Sciences of the University of Porto. There, I worked alongside Dr. Pedro Costa to develop an experimental process for creating Nitrogen-doped Carbon Quantum Dots. Currently, I am a research grant holder at INESC TEC, where I am researching and developing localization techniques using WiFi technology.
